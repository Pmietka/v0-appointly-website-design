## What "Floor Coating Leads" Really Means

Ask ten contractors what a floor coating lead is and you'll get ten answers. To some it's a name scraped off a form. To others it's a phone call from a homeowner ready to buy this week. That gap matters, because the word "lead" hides enormous differences in quality, and those differences decide whether your business grows or just spins.

A floor coating lead is only worth something if it turns into a coated floor. So before you spend a dollar chasing more leads, you need to understand what separates a lead that books from a lead that wastes your afternoon.

## The Three Tiers of Floor Coating Leads

Not all floor coating leads are created equal. They generally fall into three tiers.

**Tier 1: Cold list leads.** Names pulled from a data list or a low-quality form, often people who clicked something by accident. High volume, terrible quality. You'll burn hours dialing for almost no booked estimates.

**Tier 2: Shared marketplace leads.** A homeowner who requested quotes on a contractor-matching site, then got sold to four or five floor coating companies at once. The intent is real, but so is the price war. By the time you call, they've heard from three competitors and they're shopping you on price alone.

**Tier 3: Exclusive, qualified leads.** A homeowner who saw *your* offer, raised their hand for *you*, and isn't being sold to anyone else. These are the leads that close at a healthy margin because you're not competing on the phone before you've even seen the slab.

Most of the frustration contractors feel about "bad leads" comes from living in Tiers 1 and 2. The whole game is getting to Tier 3.

## Exclusive vs. Shared Floor Coating Leads

This is the single biggest quality lever, so it's worth being blunt about it.

When a floor coating lead is **shared**, the homeowner is comparison-shopping by design. The platform sold the same lead to your competitors on purpose. You're forced to quote scared, drop your price, and hope you're the cheapest, which is a miserable way to run a premium coating business.

When a floor coating lead is **exclusive**, the conversation flips entirely. The homeowner engaged with your brand specifically. You're not one of five calls; you're *the* call. That lets you sell on value, hold your number, and present the premium polyaspartic system instead of racing to the bottom.

For a trade where margins live and die on value selling, exclusive leads aren't a luxury. They're the difference between a profitable job and a job you wish you'd never booked.

## Quantity vs. Quality: The Trap

A lot of contractors fixate on lead *volume*. "I got 60 leads this month!" But 60 cold, shared leads that produce two booked estimates are worth far less than 20 exclusive leads that produce eight.

What actually matters is the math at the bottom of the funnel:

- How many of these leads can you reach quickly?
- How many qualify (right surface, right budget, in your service area)?
- How many turn into booked estimates?
- How many of those show up and buy?

Chase booked estimates and revenue, not raw lead count. A pile of leads is a vanity metric. Coated floors are the business.

## Why Floor Coating Leads Die (and How to Stop It)

Even great leads die, and the cause is almost always the same: **slow response.** A floor coating lead is hot for about an hour after they raise their hand. After that, they cool off, get busy, or hire the contractor who called first.

The data across home services is consistent and brutal: contacting a lead within five minutes versus thirty can multiply your booking rate several times over. Yet most contractors can't respond that fast, because they're on a job site grinding concrete when the lead comes in. The lead ages, the window closes, and they blame "bad leads" when the real problem was response time.

If you take one thing from this article: a floor coating lead's value collapses by the minute. Speed isn't a nice-to-have. It's the whole thing.
