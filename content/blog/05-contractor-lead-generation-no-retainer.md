## The Retainer Model vs Results-Based Model

Your business is built on results. You quote a job, you do the work, you get paid. Simple.

Yet when you hire a marketing agency, they flip that model. You pay first, regardless of results.

Most agencies operate on retainer. They charge a flat monthly fee whether they deliver 2 booked appointments or 20 booked appointments. Whether you close one job or zero jobs. The money comes out of your account every month like clockwork, independent of performance.

This mismatch is why so many contractors hate working with agencies.

**The Retainer Model:**
- You pay $2,000 to $5,000 per month upfront
- Agency does "marketing work" (unclear what, often)
- You may or may not get booked appointments
- You may or may not close jobs
- You pay anyway
- If you want out, there's usually an early termination fee

The agency's incentive is to keep you paying, not to deliver results. They make money whether you succeed or fail.

**The Results-Based Model (Pay-Per-Appointment):**
- You pay $0 per month in retainer
- You pay per confirmed, pre-qualified appointment when it lands on your calendar
- You only pay when something valuable was delivered
- No lock-in contracts
- The partner's incentive is to book quality appointments because if they don't, you stop using them

One model aligns incentives with the contractor. One doesn't.

## Why Contractors Are Frustrated With Agencies

Every contractor has a story.

"We hired an agency and paid them $4,000 a month for 6 months. In month 7, they said we were 'building momentum' and needed to stay committed. In month 12, we finally got some activity, but the quality wasn't there. We wasted $48,000 learning that this wasn't the right fit."

Or this one:

Or:

"We signed a 12-month contract with a $3,500 retainer. After 4 months, we wanted to cancel because we weren't happy. They said we had to pay an early termination fee. We were trapped."

The frustration comes from several things.

**Misaligned Incentives**

The agency gets paid the same whether you close 1 job or 10 jobs. They have no reason to obsess over appointment quality or your close rate. They just need to keep you signed up.

**Unclear ROI**

You never know if you're getting your money's worth. The agency will claim "brand building" or "pipeline development" when results are thin. Measuring ROI on a retainer is nearly impossible.

**Long-Term Lock-In**

If you're not happy, you're stuck. Early termination fees and contract minimums protect the agency at your expense.

**Hidden Sourcing**

Many agencies buy contacts from third parties and mark them up, or run cheap Meta ads and resell them as premium appointments. You don't know where the prospects come from or why quality varies.

**"Strategy" That Never Materializes**

Agencies sell you a comprehensive strategy involving content marketing, Google Ads, Facebook ads, and organic growth. Then they focus only on whatever is cheapest and easiest for them. Your actual strategy gets ignored.

The result: contractors feel like they're being sold, not served.

## Red Flags That a "Results-Based" Service Isn't Really Results-Based

The term "results-based" gets misused. Some agencies say they're pay-per-appointment or pay-per-results and then bury fees in the fine print.

**Red Flag 1: Hidden Retainer or Platform Fee**

They say "No retainer, just pay per appointment!" but then there's a $500 "platform fee" or "account management fee" per month.

That's a retainer. It just has a different name. Legitimate pay-per-appointment has zero monthly fees.

**Red Flag 2: Appointment Minimum or Volume Requirement**

"You must commit to 20 appointments per month minimum."

This is a retainer disguised as pay-per-appointment. If you only want 5 appointments, you still pay for 20. That's not performance-based. That's volume-based.

**Red Flag 3: Long-Term Contract Required**

"We require a 6-month commitment to get the best pricing."

If they require a long-term commitment, they're betting you won't cancel when results disappoint. Real pay-per-appointment is month-to-month. Cancel anytime.

**Red Flag 4: Vague Close-Rate Guarantees**

"We guarantee a 15% close rate on our appointments."

Nobody can guarantee your close rate. That depends on your skills, your pricing, your follow-up, and your product. The only guarantee that matters: "We deliver pre-qualified, confirmed appointments that meet the agreed criteria."

**Red Flag 5: You Don't Know the Source**

"I can't tell you exactly where appointments come from, but they're good quality."

A legitimate provider can explain their acquisition method. Are they Meta ads? Google Ads? A direct outreach team? If they won't tell you, ask harder.

**Red Flag 6: Early Termination Fee**

"If you cancel before 6 months, there's a fee."

This tells you they're betting you'll pay to get out rather than stay. That's not a partner. That's a trap.

## What Pay-For-Results Looks Like When Done Right

Here are the characteristics of a legitimate pay-per-appointment service.

**Clear Appointment Criteria**

The criteria for what constitutes a deliverable appointment is stated clearly: homeowner confirmed interest, specific service verified, location in your area, scheduled date and time.

**No Monthly Minimums or Lock-In**

You can book 1 appointment or 100 in a month. You can pause anytime. Month-to-month, no contracts.

**Transparent Acquisition Method**

They can explain exactly how they generate and qualify prospects. "We run Meta ads targeting homeowners, follow up with a qualification call, and book confirmed appointments onto your calendar."

**Pre-Qualification is Standard**

Homeowners aren't added to your calendar without a qualification call. Job type, location, intent, and timeline are confirmed before the appointment is booked.

**Fast Delivery**

Once a homeowner expresses interest and passes qualification, the appointment is booked quickly. You don't wait weeks for a calendar slot.

**Verifiable Performance Data**

They can show you anonymized data: "Contractors on our platform see an average close rate of X% on our appointments." Transparency about what you can realistically expect.

**No Long-Term Contract**

You can leave without penalty. The service works because the results speak for themselves.

## Questions to Ask Before Signing Up

Before you commit any money, get answers to these questions.

**About Acquisition Method**

- Where do your appointments come from? (Meta ads, Google, direct outreach, etc.)
- Can you walk me through exactly how a homeowner goes from initial interest to being on my calendar?
- How do you screen out people who aren't genuinely interested?

**About Appointment Quality**

- How do you define a qualified appointment?
- What happens if I show up and the homeowner doesn't remember booking?
- What's the average close rate for contractors similar to me?
- Can I speak with a reference?

**About Pricing**

- What's the exact per-appointment price?
- Are there any other fees? Setup, monthly, platform — anything?
- What constitutes a "delivered" appointment for billing purposes?

**About Flexibility**

- Can I cancel anytime?
- Is there a contract?
- Is there an early termination fee?
- Can I pause if I get too busy?

**Get It All In Writing**

Before you hand over money, get a written agreement that spells out:
- Per-appointment price
- What constitutes a qualified, delivered appointment
- Appointment delivery process
- Refund/replacement policy
- Cancellation terms
- No early termination fees

If they won't put it in writing, don't work with them.

## FAQ

**Q: If I switch to pay-per-appointment, won't I lose the marketing strategy that an agency provides?**

A: Probably yes, and it might not matter. Most retainer agencies' "strategy" is generic. If you're getting booked appointments from a good pay-per-appointment service, that IS your growth strategy. You can handle the other details (website, branding, GMB) separately. Results beat strategy talks every time.

**Q: Can I do both? Use an agency for some channels and pay-per-appointment for others?**

A: You could, but pay-per-appointment is specifically for calendar filling. If you want brand building or long-term content separately, hire a freelancer for that work — just don't pay a retainer for calendar filling.

**Q: What if pay-per-appointment quality doesn't match what an agency promises?**

A: Test it. Book 5 to 10 appointments, see what your close rate is, then compare to your previous approach. Real data beats promises every time. If the appointments don't work, you can stop immediately.

**Q: How many appointment services should I be working with?**

A: Start with one. Once you understand the quality and your close rate, you can add more volume or sources if needed. But loyalty to a service that delivers is smart.
